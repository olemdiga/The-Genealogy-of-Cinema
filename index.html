My Top 100 Favourite Movies

A curated collection of cinematic masterpieces that have left a lasting impact on me. Each film is rated on a 10-point scale with color-coded badges for quick reference.

100 Movies

The Godfather (1972)

Overview

A mafia family drama so rich it's practically marinara sauce. Loyalty, betrayal, and horse heads in beds - when Don Corleone says "family," you listen. Masterful, but the pacing might make you an offer you can't refuse... to nap.

The Dark Knight (2008)

Overview

Gotham's gritty superhero saga where chaos wears a purple suit. Explosive action, moral dilemmas, and a pencil trick you'll never forget. A bit overlong, but it's the hero we deserve.

Parasite (2019)

Overview

A darkly funny class warfare thriller that sneaks into your brain like a family into a mansion. Twists galore, from peachy schemes to bloody basements. Near flawless, but you'll need a shower after.

The Matrix (1999)

Overview

Red pill, blue pill, and bullet-time galore. This sci-fi mind-bender questions reality while delivering killer action. Dated CGI and a slightly preachy vibe don't ruin the fun.

The Platform (2019)

Overview

A vertical prison where food descends and greed ascends. Gory, grim, and a tad on-the-nose about capitalism, but it's a wild, stomach-churning ride. Bring your own snacks.

The Godfather Part II (1974)

Overview

More mafia, more betrayal, more Corleone gloom. Flips between past and present like a mobster's loyalty. Epic, but it's a long sit - grab a cannoli.

Whiplash (2014)

Overview

Drumming dreams meet a conductor from hell. Intense, sweaty, and blood-on-the-snare gripping. The ending's a crescendo, but the abuse might make you wince.

In Bruges (2008)

Overview

Hitmen hiding in a fairy-tale town, trading dark laughs and existential dread. It's a bloody good time, but the pacing's as slow as a canal boat.

IP Man (2008)

Overview

Wing Chun master punches through oppression with style. Fists fly, patriotism soars, but the plot's thinner than rice paper. Pure martial arts joy.

The Silence of the Lambs (1991)

Overview

A chilling cat-and-mouse game with a cannibalistic twist. Creepy, clever, and perfectly paced, though the lotion scene might ruin your skincare routine.

Inception (2010)

Overview

Dreams within dreams, spinning tops, and mind-bending heists. Visually stunning, but you'll need a flowchart to keep up. Still, that ending's a banger.

V for Vendetta (2005)

Overview

A masked rebel fights dystopian tyranny with flair and fireworks. Cool vibes, but the politics are as subtle as a Guy Fawkes mask at a protest.

Ford v Ferrari (2019)

Overview

Cars, egos, and a race to beat Ferrari. Revs up your pulse with thrilling tracks and corporate drama. A bit predictable, but vroom!

The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

Overview

Hope crawls through a prison sewer in this heartfelt classic. Friendship, redemption, and a poster twist that hits hard. Timeless, if a tad sentimental.

The Usual Suspects (1995)

Overview

A twisty crime tale where nobody's who they seem. That lineup scene and final reveal? Chef's kiss. A bit convoluted, but devilishly fun.

12 Angry Men (1957)

Overview

Twelve jurors, one room, and a masterclass in tension. Debates rage, biases crack, and justice hangs in the balance. Old-school, but still a knockout.

No Country for Old Men (2007)

Overview

A suitcase of cash sparks a grim, Coen Brothers chase. Bleak, brutal, and that coin toss scene haunts. Ending's divisive, but it's pure dread.

Jerusalema (2008)

Overview

A South African crime epic about a hustler's rise to power. Gritty, raw, and pulsing with Jozi's heartbeat. Uneven pacing, but it's got soul.

Hyenas (1992)

Overview

A Senegalese satire where revenge comes with a golden price tag. Sharp social commentary, but the pacing drags like a hyena with a full belly.

American Gangster (2007)

Overview

Drug lords, cops, and 1970s swagger. A sprawling crime saga that's slick but overstays its welcome. The fur coat game is strong, though.

The Big Lebowski (1998)

Overview

The Dude abides in this wacky Coen caper. A rug ties the kidnapping plot together, with bowling and White Russians for flavor. Cult classic, man.

Catch Me If You Can (2002)

Overview

A conman dances through fake identities while the FBI trips over its laces. Charming and fast-paced, but the cat-and-mouse game overstays a tad.

Rocky (1976)

Overview

An underdog boxer swings for glory and love. Heart-pounding montages and raw Philly grit. A bit cheesy, but that final fight's a champ.

12 Years a Slave (2013)

Overview

A free man's descent into slavery's horrors. Brutal, unflinching, and beautifully shot, it's a gut-punch that lingers. Heavy, but it should be.

Everything Everywhere All at Once (2022)

Overview

A multiverse mess of laundry, taxes, and hotdog fingers. Wildly creative and heartfelt, but the chaos might give you whiplash. Rocks included.

Bohemian Rhapsody (2018)

Overview

Rock anthems and stage swagger in a biopic that hits the high notes. Formulaic and polished, but that Live Aid scene will have you stomping.

Training Day (2001)

Overview

A rookie cop's day with a crooked mentor. Gritty, tense, and morally murky, with LA's streets as a character. Slightly overcooked, but juicy.

Soul (2020)

Overview

A jazz musician's soul-searching trip through life and beyond. Gorgeous, profound, and surprisingly deep for a cartoon. A bit preachy, but soulful.

Hidden Figures (2016)

Overview

Math whizzes break barriers at NASA. Uplifting and smart, but it plays safe with history's rough edges. Still, those calculations soar.

Green Book (2018)

Overview

A road trip through racism with heart and humor. Feel-good vibes clash with oversimplified history, but the journey's worth it.

Constantine (2005)

Overview

Demon-hunting with a side of snark. Cool visuals and occult vibes, but the plot's as murky as a hell portal. Still, it's a smoky ride.

King Richard (2021)

Overview

A dad's relentless drive to make tennis champs. Inspiring, if a bit hagiographic. The court scenes ace, but the drama's a bit safe.

The Way of the Dragon (1972)

Overview

Kung fu in Rome with a Colosseum showdown. Cheesy dialogue, epic fights, and pure retro charm. Plot's thin, but those nunchucks sing.

John Wick (2014)

Overview

A hitman avenges his dog and steals our hearts. Sleek, stylish, and stupidly fun action. The body count's high, but so's the cool factor.

The Accountant (2016)

Overview

A number-crunching hitman balances books and bodies. Quirky, action-packed, and a bit absurd, but the math adds up to fun.

Black Panther (2018)

Overview

Wakanda's king battles for the throne in a vibrant superhero epic. Stunning visuals, but the CGI fights can feel like a video game.

Ready Player One (2018)

Overview

A virtual reality treasure hunt stuffed with 80s nostalgia. Fun, flashy, but shallow as a pixel. The pop culture overload's a blast, though.

Creed (2015)

Overview

Rocky's legacy lives in a young boxer's fight. Emotional punches land harder than the ring ones. A bit formulaic, but it's got heart.

Cry Freedom (1987)

Overview

A journalist exposes apartheid's ugliness. Powerful, if a bit preachy, with South Africa's struggle front and center. Pacing drags at times.

Queen & Slim (2019)

Overview

A date turns into a fugitive road trip. Stylish and bold, but the social commentary's heavy-handed. Still, it's a vibe.

The Town (2010)

Overview

Bank robbers and Boston accents collide in a tense heist flick. Solid action, but the romance feels tacked on. Still, those masks are iconic.

The Banker (2020)

Overview

Black entrepreneurs outsmart racism in the 60s. Inspiring, but plays it too safe. The financial schemes are the real star.

Five Fingers for Marseilles (2017)

Overview

A South African Western with gritty heart. Outlaws and redemption clash, but the pacing's as dusty as the plains. Unique, though.

Coach Carter (2005)

Overview

A coach whips a basketball team into shape, on and off the court. Uplifting, predictable, but those game scenes slam dunk.

The One (2001)

Overview

Law Abiding Citizen (2009)

Overview

A vigilante outsmarts the justice system with traps and rage. Thrilling, but the logic's shakier than a courtroom bomb.

Overview

8 Mile (2002)

Overview

A United Kingdom (2016)

Overview

The Karate Kid (1984)

Overview

Wax on, wax off, and kick your way to glory. Pure 80s underdog magic with crane kicks and heart. Dated, but iconic.

Ocean's 11 (2001)

Overview

A slick heist with style to spare. Casinos, cons, and a twisty plan that's pure fun. A bit too cool for its own good, but it steals your heart.

Interstellar (2014)

Overview

Space, time, and cornfields in a sci-fi epic. Heart-wrenching and brain-bending, but the science gets wobbly. That bookshelf scene, though.

Overview

Overview
